## Why It Matters

Design is where large-system modernization becomes intentional instead of reactive. This phase defines the business capabilities, boundaries, flows, interfaces, modernization patterns, and agent roles that guide everything downstream.

## Key Decisions

- What are the bounded contexts, capabilities, and seams for modernization?
- What inputs, outputs, events, screens, and workflows define the domain?
- What modernization patterns apply to each part of the system?
- What roles will humans and agents play in the target process?
- What artifacts are required for implementation and governance?

## Major Activities

- Run DDD and event storming workshops
- Document user journeys, screens, inputs, outputs, and events
- Define bounded contexts and integration patterns
- Select target architectures and modernization patterns
- Design agent roles, handoffs, and review requirements
