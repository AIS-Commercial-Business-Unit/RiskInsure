## Why It Matters

Without governance, agentic delivery creates unmanaged operational, security, compliance, and business risk. Governance provides the boundaries, approvals, evidence, and escalation design that let autonomy scale inside enterprise controls.

## Executive Questions

- What use cases are permitted for agentic execution?
- What level of autonomy is acceptable by risk tier?
- What requires human approval, escalation, or exception handling?
- What tools, environments, and data may each agent access?
- What evidence must be preserved for audit, compliance, and incident review?

## Key Decisions

- Risk-tier each agentic use case based on impact, reversibility, and data sensitivity
- Define allowed actions, prohibited actions, and approval thresholds
- Assign accountable business, technical, risk, and operational owners
- Set identity, access, and blast-radius boundaries for agents and tools
- Decide how autonomy can expand as control evidence matures

## Model Components

- Risk & use-case governance
- Human accountability & decision rights
- Technical controls & assurance
- Transparency, evidence & compliance
- Workforce enablement & responsible use
- Learning & governance adaptation

## Major Activities

- Create AI and agent acceptable-use policies
- Map approval points across design, build, release, operate, and evolve
- Implement policy as code, least privilege, and tool guardrails
- Establish audit trails, decision logs, and compliance evidence packs
- Run governance reviews after incidents, escalations, or control failures
