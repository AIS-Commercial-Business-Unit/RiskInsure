## Why It Matters

Most organizations want to learn this model while delivering with it. People and enablement make the difference between a successful pilot and an enduring operating capability that clients can run themselves.

## Executive Questions

- What roles need to change as agentic delivery matures?
- How will teams learn to supervise, review, and collaborate with agents?
- What communities, coaching, and change practices are required?
- How do we preserve deep engineering and domain judgment while increasing automation?
- How will adoption and proficiency be measured?

## Key Decisions

- Define the future-state role model for business, engineering, platform, and operations
- Choose the training, coaching, and paired-delivery approach
- Set learning paths for developers, architects, reviewers, and operators
- Establish communities of practice and stewardship roles
- Determine how adoption and proficiency will be measured and reinforced

## Model Components

- Role evolution
- Training and coaching
- Change management
- Community of practice
- Stewardship and ownership
- Adoption measurement

## Major Activities

- Create role-based enablement tracks
- Deliver paired implementation with client teams
- Publish playbooks, office hours, and reusable examples
- Run communities of practice around prompts, patterns, and controls
- Track usage, confidence, proficiency, and workflow adoption
