# Customer Management - Business Requirements

**Domain**: Customer  
**Version**: 1.0.0  
**Date**: February 5, 2026

---

## Business Purpose

The Customer domain serves as the **system of record for customer identity and contact information**. It manages customer profiles, contact details, and customer relationships, providing this information to other domains throughout the insurance platform.

---

## Business Capabilities

### 1. Customer Profile Management

**Capability**: Create and maintain customer profiles with unique identifiers.

**Business Rules:**
- Each customer receives a unique `CustomerId` (GUID) upon creation
- CustomerId is generated by the API during registration
- Email address must be unique across all customers
- Birth date is required for insurance rating and underwriting
- Zip code is required for territory-based rating

**Use Cases:**
- **New Customer Registration**: Customer visits website and provides basic information to start quote
- **Customer Profile Update**: Customer updates email, phone, or address information
- **Customer Lookup**: Other domains retrieve customer information by CustomerId

---

### 2. Contact Information Management

**Capability**: Store and maintain customer contact information.

**Data Captured:**
- **Email Address**: Primary communication channel (required, unique)
- **Phone Number**: Optional, for customer service contact
- **Mailing Address**: Physical address for correspondence
- **Birth Date**: Required for age-based rating
- **Zip Code**: Required for territory rating

**Business Rules:**
- Email must be valid format and unique
- Birth date must indicate customer is at least 18 years old
- Zip code must be valid US postal code (5 digits)
- Phone number follows standard US format (optional)
- Address changes are tracked with effective dates

**Communication Preferences:**
- Email preferences (marketing, transactional, policy updates)
- Preferred contact method (email, phone, mail)
- Language preference

---

### 3. Customer Lifecycle

**Status Values:**
- **Active**: Customer actively engaging with platform (quotes, policies)
- **Inactive**: Customer has no active quotes or policies
- **Suspended**: Customer access temporarily restricted
- **Closed**: Customer account permanently closed (GDPR deletion request)

**State Transitions:**
- New → Active (upon registration)
- Active → Inactive (all policies expired, no quotes for 90+ days)
- Active → Suspended (fraud investigation, non-payment)
- Any → Closed (customer request, regulatory requirement)

---

### 4. Customer Relationships (Future)

**Capability**: Track relationships between customers for household policies.

**Relationship Types:**
- Spouse/Partner
- Dependent (child under policy)
- Co-applicant
- Beneficiary

**Business Rules:**
- One customer can have multiple relationships
- Relationships are bidirectional (spouse A → spouse B)
- Relationship effective dates tracked
- Relationships affect policy grouping and billing

## Business Events

### CustomerCreated

**When**: Customer completes initial registration form

**Business Significance**: 
- Marks start of customer journey
- Enables quote creation in Rating & Underwriting
- Establishes customer identity for all downstream operations

**Key Data:**
- CustomerId (GUID)
- Email address
- Birth date
- Zip code
- Registration timestamp

**Subscribers**: None (informational event)

---

### CustomerInformationUpdated

**When**: Customer changes contact details (email, phone, address)

**Business Significance**:
- Ensures all systems have current contact information
- Triggers address verification workflows
- Updates billing and policy correspondence addresses

**Key Data:**
- CustomerId
- Fields changed (email, phone, address)
- Old values (for audit)
- New values
- Effective date of change

**Subscribers**:
- Billing (update billing address)
- Policy (update mailing address for documents)

---

### ContactInformationChanged

**When**: Email, phone, or address modified

**Business Significance**:
- Maintains data consistency across domains
- Supports compliance with data accuracy requirements

**Key Data:**
- CustomerId
- Contact type (email, phone, address)
- Previous value
- New value
- Change timestamp

---

## Business Constraints

### Data Quality Requirements

1. **Email Validation**:
   - Must be valid email format
   - Must be unique (no duplicate customers)
   - Must be verified (email confirmation link)

2. **Age Requirements**:
   - Customer must be 18+ years old to purchase insurance
   - Birth date must be reasonable (not in future, not > 120 years ago)

3. **Geographic Coverage**:
   - Currently only US zip codes supported
   - Future: Canada postal codes

### Privacy & Compliance

1. **PII Protection**:
   - Email, phone, address are Personally Identifiable Information (PII)
   - Access logged for audit purposes
   - Encryption at rest required

2. **GDPR/CCPA Compliance**:
   - Right to be forgotten (customer deletion)
   - Right to data portability (export customer data)
   - Consent tracking for marketing communications

3. **Data Retention**:
   - Active customer data retained indefinitely
   - Inactive customers: 7 years after last policy expires
   - Closed/deleted customers: Personal data purged, transactional data anonymized

## Success Metrics

- **Customer Registration Success Rate**: % of registrations completing successfully
- **Email Verification Rate**: % of customers verifying email within 24 hours
- **Profile Completeness**: % of customers with complete contact information
- **Data Accuracy**: % of email bounces, invalid addresses
- **Customer Retention**: % of customers with active policies or quotes

## Integration Points

### Outbound Dependencies

**None** - Customer domain has no dependencies on other domains

### Inbound Consumers

- **Rating & Underwriting**: Uses CustomerId, birth date, zip code for quotes
- **Policy**: Uses CustomerId for policy ownership
- **Billing**: Uses CustomerId for billing account association
- **FundsTransferMgt**: Uses CustomerId for payment method ownership
- **Premium**: Uses CustomerId for premium calculations

## Glossary

- **CustomerId**: Globally unique identifier (GUID) for a customer
- **PII**: Personally Identifiable Information (email, phone, address)
- **Active Customer**: Customer with at least one active quote or policy
- **Customer Profile**: Collection of customer demographic and contact information
